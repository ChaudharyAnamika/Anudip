The Taj Mahal

Location: Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India

Overview

The Taj Mahal is a 17th-century white marble mausoleum built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. It is one of the most famous monuments in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

History

Construction began around 1632 and was substantially complete by 1648, with finishing touches continuing for several years.

Visitor Information

  • Best time to visit: October to March.
  • Entry: Paid entry for foreign and domestic visitors.
  • Timings: Typically opens at sunrise and closes at sunset; closed on Fridays for general visitors.
  • Tip: Carry valid ID for ticket purchase.

What to See

  • Main Dome & Inner Chamber: Marble inlay work and calligraphy panels.
  • Gardens: Symmetrical charbagh garden with reflecting pools.
  • Yamuna Riverside: Lovely views of the Taj.

How to Reach

  • By Air: Agra (Kheria) airport — limited connections; Delhi is the major nearby airport.
  • By Train: Frequent trains connect Agra to major cities.
  • By Road: Well-connected by national highways.

Nearby Attractions

  1. Agra Fort — UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  2. Itmad-ud-Daulah (Baby Taj).
  3. Mehtab Bagh — great Taj views.

Practical Tips

  • Wear comfortable shoes.
  • Carry water and sun protection.
  • Aim for sunrise/late afternoon for photos.

Learn More

For official hours and ticketing, check the Archaeological Survey of India: asiatic.indianheritage.gov.in
