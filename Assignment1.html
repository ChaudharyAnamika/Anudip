The Taj Mahal

Location: Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India

Overview

The Taj Mahal is a 17th-century white marble mausoleum built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. It is one of the most famous monuments in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The Taj combines elements of Islamic, Persian, Ottoman Turkish and Indian architectural styles.

History

Construction began around 1632 and was substantially complete by 1648, with finishing touches continuing for several years. The complex includes the main mausoleum, a grand gateway, a mosque, a guest house and formal gardens laid out in the charbagh (four-part garden) style.

What to See

  • Main Dome & Inner Chamber: Marble inlay work (pietra dura) and calligraphy panels.
  • Gardens: Symmetrical charbagh garden with reflecting pools.
  • Yamuna Riverside: Lovely views of the Taj from the riverbank, especially at sunrise/sunset.
  • Red Sandstone Gateway: Imposing entrance with ornate inscriptions.

Visitor Information

  • Best time to visit: October to March (pleasant weather; sunrise visits are popular).
  • Entry: Paid entry for foreign and domestic visitors; separate fee for the main mausoleum dome access.
  • Timings: Typically opens at sunrise and closes at sunset; closed on Fridays for general visitors (check current schedule before planning).
  • Tip: Carry valid ID for ticket purchase; mobile phones and cameras are allowed but follow on-site rules.

How to Reach Agra

  • By Air: Agra has an airport (Kheria) with limited connections; major nearby airports: Delhi (Indira Gandhi International).
  • By Train: Frequent trains connect Agra to Delhi, Jaipur, Lucknow and other major cities.
  • By Road: Well-connected by national highways; Agra is about 200–250 km from Delhi depending on the route.

Nearby Attractions in Uttar Pradesh (Agra Area)

  1. Agra Fort — a UNESCO World Heritage Site near the Taj.
  2. Itmad-ud-Daulah (Baby Taj) — an exquisite tomb with delicate inlay work.
  3. Mehtab Bagh — garden complex across the Yamuna with great Taj views.

Practical Tips

  • Wear comfortable shoes — there is walking on marble and gardens.
  • Carry water and sun protection during warmer months.
  • For crowd-free photos, aim for early morning (sunrise) or late afternoon.
  • Respect local rules: no touching carved surfaces, and follow security checks.
